# Public Value and Policy Leverage

**Policy leverage** refers to how much a project advances public value beyond private financial return. This includes catalytic impact, infrastructure alignment, and correcting underinvestment.

| Site | Public Value Summary |
|------|------------------------|
| **Durham** | Highest catalytic impact due to proximity to VCU athletic village, Children’s Hospital, and the Flying Squirrels stadium. Supports walkability and mixed-use growth. |
| **Downtown** | Moderate value through infrastructure alignment and reinvestment near the Capitol. |
| **Glen Allen** | Lowest value; reinforces car-dependent sprawl despite proximity to retail. |

Durham offers the strongest combination of community benefit and long-term regional impact.
